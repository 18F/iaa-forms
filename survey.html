IAA Preparation form

Combined with a mutually agreeable statement of work, your answers to the following questions give us everything we need to draft an Interagency Agreement. Please have the following information on hand:

  • Basic information about your agency as well as the office within your agency requesting our services.
  • Basic information about your project as well as contact information for your program official (who can sign IAAs).
  • Financial information such as the type of money your agency will use (for example, “one-year,” “multi-year,” or “no-year”) as well as contact information for your finance office’s point of contact (who can ensure that payments to 18F are accurate and timely) and funding official (who can obligate funds).

All fields are required unless otherwise indicated. For additional context on this form, see our Primer on Interagency Agreements.

Program official

The program official is a member of your agency who has authority to sign the IAA. This person will also ensure that our work is properly scoped. This person may or may not be your agency’s contracting officer depending on its IAA business process.

Funding official

The funding official is the person in your office or organization who has the authority to obligate funds (this person will sign Treasury form 7600B). Usually this is someone in the Office of the Chief Financial Officer.
